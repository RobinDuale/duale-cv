Mandates · External growth

Build-up and external growth.
Creating value after closing.

An acquisition does not create value at signing. It creates value when teams, offers, tools, customers and commercial priorities are integrated without breaking business momentum.

Robin Dualé has integrated 5 acquisitions and managed multi-entity portfolios in B2B SaaS, Data, professional training and information services environments. His approach to build-up is operational: turning external growth logic into a coherent, readable and scalable organization.

What is at stake after closing

The post-acquisition phase is often more complex than the transaction itself. Teams need to understand what changes, what does not, who decides, how priorities are arbitrated and how customers will be served. Without that clarity, synergies remain theoretical.

  • Secure key teams and strategic customers
  • Clarify governance and the role of the acquired company's management
  • Align offers, brands, pricing and commercial priorities
  • Integrate tools, reporting, finance, HR and IT systems
  • Identify realistic synergies without disrupting daily execution
  • Create a shared management rhythm between existing entities and acquired companies

External and organic growth must reinforce each other

A successful build-up is not about stacking companies. It must strengthen the value proposition, open new markets, accelerate organic growth and improve revenue quality. The acquisition must become a commercial, product or operational accelerator — not an additional layer of complexity.

Multi-entity experience

At WEKA France, Robin Dualé managed 7 operating companies, €50M in revenue and 300 employees, with 5 acquisitions integrated and 6 exit processes completed. This experience provides concrete insight into the human, commercial, IT, financial and organizational challenges that follow an acquisition.

Key takeaways
  • Build-up creates value through integration, not through the announcement.
  • Synergies must be translated into measurable operational priorities.
  • External growth must reinforce organic growth, product, go-to-market or revenue quality.
  • The CEO must protect entrepreneurial energy while structuring governance and management.
