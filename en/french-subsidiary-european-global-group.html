Leading a French subsidiary.
Local autonomy, European or global alignment.

Robin Dualé has led French entities within European or global structures, with local operational responsibility, shareholder dialogue, reporting to a holding company or international group, and execution in transformation, growth or post-acquisition integration contexts.

The point is not to claim international operations where they do not exist. The point is different: knowing how to make a French company succeed within European, global or corporate governance, with group priorities, reporting expectations and sometimes a matrix culture.

The challenges of a CEO France or Managing Director France

  • Align local strategy with European or global group priorities
  • Maintain operational autonomy while respecting matrix governance
  • Integrate a French company after acquisition without losing clients or key talent
  • Translate board, headquarters or shareholder expectations into a local execution plan
  • Structure growth, go-to-market, product and operations in the French context
  • Build trust between local teams and international governance

Operate locally, report globally

A French subsidiary often succeeds through a fragile balance. Too much autonomy can create disconnection from the group. Too much centralization can break local momentum. The leader's role is to hold both requirements: deliver in France and make execution readable for European or global governance.

Experience in European and global structures

Within WEKA Group, Robin Dualé led the French activities inside a European structure, with multi-entity management, shareholder reporting and interaction with the holding company. At Quick-FDS / 3E Group, he led post-acquisition integration into a global group backed by New Mountain Capital, in an international and matrix environment.
